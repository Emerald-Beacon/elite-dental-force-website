The Problem

Eligibility Is Still Too Hard to Trust

Most dental offices verify eligibility the same way they did fifteen years ago: manual portal lookups, phone holds, and staff entering data they are not confident is accurate. The result is front-desk friction, billing surprises, and claims that leave money on the table before the patient leaves the chair.

Still Manual

Eligibility lookups rely on staff time, portal access, and manual data entry. High-volume days mean steps get skipped.

Payer-Dependent

Response quality, frequency limitations, and data format vary by payer. The same question gets different answers depending on who you ask and how.

Fragmented Data

Benefit information lives in the PMS, the payer portal, and staff memory. There is no single source of truth for what a patient's plan actually covers.

Late in the Workflow

When eligibility is verified at checkout or after the claim is submitted, the window to correct issues has already closed.

What It Does

Eligibility Intelligence
Built Around the Appointment

EDiFi Eligibility Standalone organizes eligibility work around the appointment schedule rather than the billing queue. The goal is to surface relevant benefit information before the patient arrives, not after.

Appointment-driven eligibility workflow Eligibility checks organized by the office schedule, not a manual trigger list.
Benefit readiness view See which patients on the schedule have verified benefit information and which need attention before the visit.
Payer and plan visibility Surface available plan details, coverage type, and coordination of benefits information in one view.
Historical usage insights Where available, surface benefit usage history to support treatment planning conversations.
Eligibility explanation layer Instead of hiding uncertainty, surface confidence levels, data gaps, and what the office should verify manually.
Built for front office, billing, and RCM teams Designed for the staff who own eligibility, not just billing administrators reviewing reports after the fact.

Current Integration

EDiFi Eligibility Standalone is currently being validated through a focused Open Dental pilot workflow. The pilot is testing the appointment-driven sync path, benefit data accuracy, and front-office usability.

Broader PMS expansion is part of the product roadmap. EDF has not committed to specific timelines or additional integrations at this stage.

About Data Accuracy Eligibility data quality depends on payer response quality, PMS data availability, and office configuration. EDiFi is being designed to surface confidence levels, data gaps, and explanation context rather than presenting uncertain data as definitive. The system is not a substitute for payer verification when benefit accuracy is critical to treatment acceptance.
